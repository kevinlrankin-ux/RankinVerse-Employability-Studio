## Policy summary

Professional workplaces require conduct that protects dignity, safety, and equal treatment. Harassment and sexual harassment are serious workplace issues. Employees should understand what prohibited conduct is, how reporting works, and that retaliation for protected reporting or participation in a process is not acceptable.

---

## Core points

### 1. Harassment

Harassment is unwelcome conduct that may violate workplace rules or legal protections when it is based on a protected characteristic and is severe, frequent, or otherwise serious enough to affect the work environment or employment conditions.

### 2. Sexual harassment

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when the conduct affects employment, interferes with work performance, or contributes to an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

### 3. Professional conduct expectations

Employees are expected to maintain professional conduct, respect boundaries, and avoid behavior that creates an unsafe, intimidating, degrading, or offensive work environment.

### 4. Reporting

Workplaces should have procedures for reporting prohibited conduct. Employees should understand how to report concerns through the appropriate channel.

### 5. Non-retaliation

Retaliation for reporting prohibited conduct, opposing unlawful practices, or participating in an investigation or proceeding is not acceptable.

---

## Workplace examples

Examples should remain age-appropriate, direct, and workplace-specific.

Appropriate examples may include:
- repeated unwelcome comments of a sexual nature
- inappropriate touching
- repeated offensive remarks after being told to stop
- conduct that creates an intimidating or hostile environment
- negative action taken against a person because they reported misconduct

Do not use stylized, joking, or ambiguous examples in this document.

---

## Student use

Students should be able to explain:

- what prohibited conduct means in this context
- why reporting matters
- what non-retaliation means
- how professional conduct supports workplace safety and dignity
