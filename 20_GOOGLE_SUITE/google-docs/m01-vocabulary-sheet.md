## Purpose

This vocabulary sheet supports Module 01 by defining basic workplace rights, employer responsibilities, employee responsibilities, reporting, and hazard-related language.

---

## Terms and definitions

### worker right
A protection or entitlement a worker has under workplace rules, safety standards, or applicable law.

### employer responsibility
A duty the employer has to provide required protections, training, procedures, and workplace conditions consistent with applicable requirements.

### employee responsibility
A duty the worker has to follow instructions, use training, follow procedures, and report unsafe conditions or incidents through the proper process.

### hazard
A condition, action, material, or situation that can cause injury, illness, damage, or loss.

### unsafe condition
A workplace condition that increases risk of harm, injury, or damage.

### reporting
The act of informing the appropriate person, office, or system about a hazard, unsafe condition, injury, or prohibited conduct.

### procedure
A required or expected way of completing a task or responding to a situation.

### training
Instruction provided so workers understand required practices, procedures, and expectations.

### compliance
Following required rules, standards, procedures, or policies.

### responsibility
An obligation to act appropriately, follow expectations, and carry out assigned duties.

### corrective action
Steps taken to address a problem, reduce risk, or prevent the problem from happening again.

### accountability
Being answerable for actions, decisions, and compliance with expectations.

---

## Student use

Students should be able to:

- identify the difference between a worker right and an employer responsibility
- explain why reporting matters
- describe one employee responsibility related to safety or conduct
- use these terms accurately during discussion or written reflection
