# Workplace Harassment and Sexual Harassment
# Status: Draft v0.1
# Module ID: M03

---

## Page purpose

Provide dry, policy-style instruction on prohibited conduct, reporting expectations, employer response, and anti-retaliation.

---

## Why this matters

Professional workplaces require conduct that protects dignity, safety, and equal treatment. Harassment and sexual harassment are serious workplace issues. Workers should understand what prohibited conduct is, how reporting works, and that retaliation for protected reporting is not acceptable.

---

## What you will learn

- definitions related to workplace harassment and sexual harassment
- examples of prohibited conduct
- reporting expectations
- non-retaliation basics
- expected standards of professional conduct

---

## Core lesson sections

### 1. Definitions

This section should use source-grounded definitions from the uploaded Title VII and sexual harassment materials.

### 2. Prohibited conduct

This section should explain that unwelcome conduct, including conduct of a sexual nature, may violate workplace standards and legal protections depending on the conduct and circumstances.

### 3. Workplace examples

Examples should be age-appropriate, workplace-specific, and direct. Avoid stylized or ambiguous examples.

### 4. Reporting procedures

Students should understand that workplace procedures exist for reporting prohibited conduct and that concerns should be reported through the appropriate channel.

### 5. Non-retaliation

Students should understand that retaliation for protected reporting or participation in an investigation is prohibited.
