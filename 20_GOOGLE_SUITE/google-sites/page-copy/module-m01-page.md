# Worker Rights and Employer Responsibilities
# Status: Draft v0.1
# Module ID: M01

---

## Page purpose

Introduce basic worker rights, employer responsibilities, employee responsibilities, and reporting protections.

---

## Why this matters

Workplaces operate under formal responsibilities. Workers have rights. Employers have responsibilities. Employees are also expected to follow workplace rules, use training, and report hazards or unsafe conditions appropriately.

---

## What you will learn

- basic worker rights
- employer responsibilities
- employee responsibilities
- why reporting matters
- how protections support workplace safety

---

## Core lesson sections

### 1. Worker rights

Workers have the right to a workplace that follows applicable safety and health requirements. Workers also have the right to raise concerns about hazards and unsafe conditions.

### 2. Employer responsibilities

Employers are responsible for providing workplaces that meet applicable requirements, providing necessary training, and taking reasonable steps to reduce workplace hazards.

### 3. Employee responsibilities

Employees are expected to follow training, use equipment properly, follow established procedures, and report hazards, injuries, and unsafe conditions through the appropriate process.

### 4. Reporting and protection

Reporting is part of workplace safety and accountability. Workers should understand that reporting systems exist to reduce risk and support corrective action.
