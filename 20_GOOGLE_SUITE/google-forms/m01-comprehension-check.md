## Questions

### 1. Which statement best describes a worker right?
Type: Multiple choice

Options:
- A task assigned by a supervisor
- A protection or entitlement recognized in the workplace
- A personal preference about work hours
- A reward for high performance

Correct answer:
- A protection or entitlement recognized in the workplace

---

### 2. Which item is an employer responsibility?
Type: Multiple choice

Options:
- Ignoring reported hazards
- Providing required training and workplace protections
- Refusing to document injuries
- Allowing any procedure to be skipped

Correct answer:
- Providing required training and workplace protections

---

### 3. Which item is an employee responsibility?
Type: Multiple choice

Options:
- Following procedures and reporting unsafe conditions
- Rewriting company policy independently
- Deciding not to use required equipment
- Ignoring training after orientation

Correct answer:
- Following procedures and reporting unsafe conditions

---

### 4. What is a hazard?
Type: Multiple choice

Options:
- A workplace condition or situation that can cause harm
- A break in the schedule
- A personal disagreement
- A performance bonus

Correct answer:
- A workplace condition or situation that can cause harm

---

### 5. Why does reporting matter?
Type: Checkbox

Options:
- It helps identify risks
- It supports corrective action
- It can help reduce future harm
- It should only be used after a serious injury
- It is part of workplace accountability

Correct answers:
- It helps identify risks
- It supports corrective action
- It can help reduce future harm
- It is part of workplace accountability

---

### 6. Short response
Type: Short answer

Prompt:
Name one worker right and one employer responsibility.

Expected response guidance:
Student should identify one reasonable example of each.

---

### 7. Short response
Type: Short answer

Prompt:
Why is it important for employees to follow procedures?

Expected response guidance:
Student should connect procedures to safety, consistency, compliance, or reduced risk.
