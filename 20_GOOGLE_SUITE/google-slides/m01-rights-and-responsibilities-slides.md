## Slide 2 - Why this matters
- workplaces operate under formal expectations
- workers have rights
- employers have responsibilities
- employees also have responsibilities
- reporting helps reduce risk

Speaker note:
Keep this opening direct and practical.

---

## Slide 3 - Learning objectives
By the end of this module, students should be able to:
- identify basic worker rights
- identify employer responsibilities
- identify employee responsibilities
- explain why reporting matters

---

## Slide 4 - Worker rights
- workers have rights related to workplace safety and fair treatment
- workers can raise concerns about hazards and unsafe conditions
- workers should understand that reporting is part of workplace accountability

---

## Slide 5 - Employer responsibilities
- provide required training
- provide required protections
- reduce or control hazards where required
- maintain procedures and expectations

---

## Slide 6 - Employee responsibilities
- follow procedures
- use training properly
- use required equipment properly
- report hazards, incidents, and unsafe conditions appropriately

---

## Slide 7 - Hazard and unsafe condition
- a hazard is something that can cause harm
- an unsafe condition is a workplace condition that increases risk
- identifying risk early supports corrective action

---

## Slide 8 - Reporting
- reporting helps identify problems
- reporting supports corrective action
- reporting is part of a safe and accountable workplace
- reporting should follow the appropriate channel

---

## Slide 9 - Quick scenario
Scenario:
A worker notices an unsafe condition near a common work area.

Prompt:
What should happen next?

Suggested discussion points:
- follow reporting procedure
- avoid ignoring known risk
- support corrective action

---

## Slide 10 - Key takeaways
- rights and responsibilities work together
- safety depends on procedure and reporting
- employers and employees both have defined roles
