## Slide 2 - Why this matters
- professional workplaces require conduct that protects dignity and safety
- prohibited conduct can harm people and workplace culture
- employees should understand reporting procedures and non-retaliation basics

Speaker note:
Keep tone formal and direct.

---

## Slide 3 - Learning objectives
By the end of this module, students should be able to:
- identify prohibited conduct in this context
- describe basic reporting expectations
- explain non-retaliation in plain language
- describe expected professional conduct

---

## Slide 4 - Harassment
- harassment is unwelcome conduct that may violate workplace rules or legal protections
- conduct may be serious based on nature, pattern, severity, or effect on the work environment

---

## Slide 5 - Sexual harassment
- sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances
- it can include requests for sexual favors
- it can include other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature
- it may affect work conditions or the work environment

---

## Slide 6 - Professional conduct expectations
- respect boundaries
- avoid inappropriate comments or conduct
- maintain a professional environment
- support dignity and equal treatment

---

## Slide 7 - Reporting
- workplaces should have a reporting procedure
- employees should know the appropriate reporting channel
- concerns should be reported promptly and appropriately

---

## Slide 8 - Non-retaliation
- retaliation is not acceptable
- negative action because a person reported misconduct or participated in a process is prohibited
- reporting systems only work when retaliation is not tolerated

---

## Slide 9 - Workplace examples
Use direct, age-appropriate examples such as:
- repeated unwelcome comments
- inappropriate touching
- offensive conduct after being told to stop
- negative treatment after a report is made

Speaker note:
Do not use joking, stylized, or ambiguous examples.

---

## Slide 10 - Key takeaways
- prohibited conduct is serious
- reporting matters
- retaliation is not acceptable
- professionalism includes respect, boundaries, and accountability
