## 1. Site Purpose

The Google Site serves as the main front door for **RankinVerse Employability Studio**.

Its purpose is to:
- provide a clean entry point for each student
- organize all modules in a clear sequence
- connect students to Slides, Docs, Forms, and Sheets assets
- separate student-facing pages from facilitator-facing pages
- maintain a serious, workplace-oriented tone, especially for compliance content

## 4. Global Page Rules

### All pages should:
- use consistent section headings
- use clear page titles
- keep compliance language plain and formal
- embed or link only the assets relevant to that page
- make student next steps obvious
- avoid clutter
- support mobile and laptop viewing

### Compliance pages must:
- avoid playful language
- avoid decorative narrative interruptions
- maintain neutral formatting
- use clear definitions and procedures
- separate “what the rule is” from “what the student should do”

---

## 5. Page-by-Page Structure

### Home
Provide identity, welcome copy, and a Start button.

### Start Here
Explain the course flow, expectations, and how to launch or resume.

### Modules
Act as the module directory and main navigation page.

### Scenarios
Central place for branching decision scenarios and case-based learning.

### Progress
Show students what modules are complete, in progress, or next.

### Resources
Collect approved student-facing references, downloads, and support materials.

### RankinVerse Guides
Provide visual and identity layer without interfering with compliance content.

### Facilitator Portal
Provide instructors with implementation notes, pacing, source registry, and deployment materials.

---

## 6. Launch Flow Through the Site

### Initial student flow
1. Student opens site
2. Student lands on `Home`
3. Student selects `Start Here`
4. Student launches or resumes assigned workspace
5. Student completes Phase 1 modules in order
6. Progress page updates central tracking
